## PART 2 — THE LEARNING METHODOLOGY

Five principles drawn from second-language acquisition research, each wired into a specific product feature:

### 1. Spaced Repetition (Ebbinghaus, Pimsleur intervals)
Vocabulary and phrases resurface at scientifically-spaced intervals to move words from short-term to long-term memory.
→ **Product feature:** Every lesson ends with a *Recall Cycle* — yesterday's words, last week's phrases, last month's structures. Not a separate flashcard app.

### 2. Comprehensible Input +1 (Krashen)
Content is pitched slightly above current ability — challenging enough to force growth, accessible enough to understand most of it.
→ **Product feature:** Adaptive lesson pathing. A placement test sets your level, then each week's content recalibrates based on performance.

### 3. Output + Feedback (Swain's Output Hypothesis)
Learners must *produce* language, not just recognize it. Speaking and writing under mild pressure, with feedback, is where real acquisition happens.
→ **Product feature:** Daily speaking drills — record your response, get AI feedback on pronunciation, fluency, and accuracy. Weekly "conversation sims" with branching dialogue.

### 4. Task-Based Learning (Long, Ellis)
Lessons organized around real-world tasks, not abstract grammar rules. Grammar emerges from use.
→ **Product feature:** Every unit has a *mission* — "order food at a Karak café," "explain your weekend in the majlis," "disagree politely in a meeting." You graduate the unit by completing the mission.

### 5. Interleaving & Desirable Difficulty (Bjork)
Mixing old + new material, and making retrieval slightly effortful, dramatically improves long-term retention — even though it feels harder in the moment.
→ **Product feature:** Deliberately non-smooth review. Mixed-topic quizzes, no "word of the day" hand-holding, increasing cognitive load over weeks.

## PART 3 — PROGRAM ARCHITECTURE

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Duration | Outcome (CEFR) | You Can |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Basic** | 3 months | A1 → A2 | Introduce yourself, shop, order food, give directions, hold basic conversations with patient speakers |
| **Intermediate** | 6 months | A2 → B1 | Discuss work, opinions, plans, past events; understand slow Khaleeji media; navigate social situations confidently |
| **Expert** | 12 months | B1 → B2+ | Debate, negotiate, understand fast speech, use nuanced idioms, read Emirati news, function in professional settings |

Each tier is self-contained — a Basic user gets real outcomes even if they never upgrade.

### Graduation Moments

At the end of each tier, the user completes a **Fluency Demonstration**:
- Recorded conversation with an Emirati speaker (or AI conversation sim)
- Comprehension test on authentic Emirati media
- Written/spoken task representative of the level

They receive a **certificate + shareable progress report**. This is the moment they feel "I actually learned this." Huge for retention, reviews, and word of mouth.

### Core Feature Set

**Learning**
- Adaptive lesson pathing (SR + CI+1 algorithm)
- Dialect-native audio library (speakers across ages, genders, emirates)
- AI speaking practice with pronunciation + fluency scoring
- Weekly mission-based units (task-based learning)
- Recall Cycles at the end of every lesson (spaced repetition)
- Cultural context cards — when/how/why to use what you just learned

**Measurement**
- CEFR-aligned progress tracking (not just XP)
- Skills mastered dashboard (verbs conjugated, phrases produced, hours of CI)
- Weekly progress email summarizing real gains
- Graduation certificates with shareable results

**Cultural Depth**
- Majlis etiquette module
- Business Arabic module
- Regional variation notes (Abu Dhabi vs Dubai vs Sharjah vs Northern Emirates)
- Guest content from Emirati speakers, poets, radio hosts

**Engagement (intentionally restrained)**
- No streaks that punish missed days
- No fake urgency timers
- Weekly goals you set (not daily goals forced on you)
- Optional social: share progress with a study partner
