## Done since the 2026-04-21 audit

These items from the audit's "smallest set of moves" list are shipped:

Also built (beyond the audit's to-do list, but called for by the spec):

- **Level gating** — `isLevelUnlocked(levelId, { placementBand, completedMissionIds })` gates the Library by placement band OR ≥3 completed missions at the prior level.
- **Graduation assessment** — `/graduation/[levelId]` runs an 8-question MCQ check at 80% pass, writes the next CEFR band on success.
- **CI+1 band filtering** — `buildTodaysQueue` caps new-card draws at learner's band + 1 step, so an A2 placement doesn't see B1 phrases on day one.

Test coverage for all of the above: 183 tests across 6 suites, all passing.

## What remains — the rest of the spec

Ordered by leverage-per-effort.

### Milestone 1 — Content parity (high leverage, content work)

The mechanics are built but the levels above A1 are empty rooms.

- **A2 mission pack** — 5 scenarios (e.g. ordering at a mall, scheduling a meeting, negotiating rent, describing a weekend, giving directions back). Each needs ~12 phrases and 4+ quiz items. Unblocks the graduation-to-A2 path for the first time.
- **B1 mission pack** — 5 scenarios (business email in Arabic, weekend plans with friends, doctor's visit, renting a car, explaining your job).
- **B2 mission pack** — 5 scenarios (panel discussion, negotiating a contract, media commentary, nuanced apology, storytelling).

Effort: ~1 week of content work per band, with dialect review by a native Emirati speaker. No code changes unless new quiz types are introduced.

### Milestone 2 — Task-based learning beyond MCQ

`QuizQuestion.type` already accepts `'mcq' | 'arrange' | 'fillBlank' | 'translate'`, but only `mcq` is rendered.

- Build renderers for `arrange` (drag-to-order words), `fillBlank` (cloze with input), and `translate` (free-text with Levenshtein-tolerant comparison).
- Mix types inside a single graduation run so the check is task-based, not trivia-based.

Effort: ~3–5 days per renderer; scoring logic is the hard part for `translate`.

### Milestone 3 — Output + feedback (speaking practice)

This is the biggest missing SLA pillar.

- Record learner audio with `expo-av` (already a peer of `expo-speech`; small add).
- Device-side STT via `expo-speech-recognition` or a cloud call to Whisper for better dialect handling.
- Score against expected Arabic text with a phoneme-distance metric; surface "try again" vs "nailed it."
- Gate mission completion on at least one successful speaking rep per mission.

Effort: ~2 weeks end-to-end. Crude scoring is acceptable per the audit — "it's the output loop that matters."

### Milestone 4 — Cultural depth modules

- **Majlis etiquette module** — 1 unit covering greetings by age/status, seating conventions, coffee service, when to leave.
- **Business Arabic module** — 1 unit on email sign-offs, phone protocol, meeting cadence.
- **Regional variation cards** — short notes on AUH vs DXB vs Northern Emirates dialect differences layered on top of existing phrases.
- **Guest content slot** — audio clips from actual Emirati speakers for at least the A1 mission phrases, starting to displace TTS.

Effort: heavily content-bound. A week per module once a dialect advisor is engaged.

### Milestone 5 — Program / tier architecture

The spec's 3-month / 6-month / 12-month tiered program doesn't exist as architecture.

- Define the tier gate (Basic = A1→A2, Intermediate = A2→B1, Expert = B1→B2).
- Paywall stub at tier boundaries — RevenueCat SDK is not yet wired, but `revenuecat-key.json` is in the repo.
- "Your program" surface on the You tab showing tier, weeks in, expected graduation window.

Effort: ~1 week code, plus whatever IAP/pricing work is needed outside the app.

### Milestone 6 — Graduation moments that mean something

Current graduation is an 8-question MCQ. The spec calls for a "Fluency Demonstration."

- Record a free-form conversation against a scenario prompt; save the audio.
- Comprehension check on authentic media (a real clip, not a scripted quiz).
- Generate a PDF certificate with the learner's name, CEFR band, and date.

Effort: ~1 week once Milestone 3 (speaking) lands. The PDF is trivial (`expo-print` or a cloud render).

### Milestone 7 — Measurement beyond mastered-count

- **Skills-mastered dashboard** — break "mastered" into verbs conjugated, phrases produced, hours of comprehensible input.
- **Weekly progress email** — `notificationService.ts` is wired for a daily push; extend with a weekly email digest on a server, or keep it in-app as a weekly notification summary.
- **Weekly recalibration** — a short 3-question check every 7 days that nudges `placementBand` up or down based on recent performance, instead of relying solely on graduation.

Effort: ~1 week for the dashboard, more if email goes out-of-app (needs server).

### Milestone 8 — Marketing / store surface

- Landing page (web) aligned with the spec's copy. Only `privacy-policy/index.html` exists today.
- Store copy refresh — the "3/6/12-month program" claim can't truthfully be made until Milestone 5.

Effort: ~1 week if a designer is involved.

## Suggested sequencing

If I were shipping this, I'd cut it into three releases:

**v2.1 — "Content fills the frame" (≈3 weeks)**
Milestones 1 + 2. Adds A2 content and non-MCQ quiz types. The CEFR gating starts paying off.

**v2.2 — "Speak and be heard" (≈3 weeks)**
Milestone 3 + a slice of Milestone 6 (speaking reps required to complete missions; certificate at A1 graduation). Closes the single biggest SLA gap.

**v2.3 — "A program, not an app" (≈4 weeks)**
Milestones 4 + 5 + 7. Cultural modules, tier architecture, measurement dashboard. This is when the product matches the spec's positioning.

B1/B2 content (rest of Milestone 1) and the web/store work (Milestone 8) run in parallel with all of the above as content/marketing tracks.
